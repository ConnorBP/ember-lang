## How to read this report

Every finding below is paired with the **fix that closes it**. A finding without a concrete, implementable fix is incomplete and is not shipped here. Findings are classified:

- **Still-missing piece of a fixed item** — the fix shipped for a 07-09 finding left a reachable code path uncovered.
- **New boundary defect** — the remediation introduced a new attack/edge surface.
- **Regression** — the remediation broke something that worked at `381ed76`.
- **Pre-existing** — present before `baa90c1`, surfaced or broadened by the new work.

Evidence labels: **Reproduced** (executable probe under `tmp_edit/` triggered it against a freshly-built/freshly-linked binary), **Confirmed by inspection** (cited control/data flow), **Contract-gated** (unsafe only when a documented contract is violated).

## Executive summary

A fresh clean Release build completed **20/20 CTest** in ~19s and the language suite returned **217 passed, 0 failed, 0 skipped**. `ext_sync` passed **20/20** consecutive runs (the 07-09 nondeterminism is gone). These were re-run from a fresh out-of-tree build, not the committed message.

The re-audit confirmed the remediation's core claims hold under independent scrutiny: W^X, B1 context isolation, function-reference provenance, `PERM_FFI` gating, atomic dispatch, registry-base stability, and the 07-09 Critical/High fixes (loader bounds, cast matrix, handle barriers, division traps, `r13`/`r14` preservation, `do-while`/`sizeof`/`offsetof`, f64, unsigned/narrow semantics) all still pass independent probes. The previously-deferred items — H12 portability, H14 export signatures, M3 reclamation, M4 budget, M5 lexical defer, and the three §10 static-analysis fixes — are **substantively implemented, not circularly self-tested**. Fresh cross-process, concurrency, and direct-JIT probes validated each.

It also found **0 Critical, 3 High, 7 Medium, 1 Low** gaps, every one with a concrete closing fix. The most serious:

1. **M4 `while`-`continue` bypasses the loop budget charge** — a 1,000,000-iteration `while` with `continue` completes under a budget of 20 (High, still-missing piece of M4).
2. **M4 coarse per-statement charging cannot bound reachable straight-line work** — a 2,000-term expression, 300 native calls, or 64 KiB of aggregate copies run under budgets of 5/3/30 (High, still-missing piece of M4).
3. **§10(1) narrow typed literal fold bypasses target-width normalization** — `1073741824_i32 << 1` folds to `2147483648` while the runtime path normalizes to `-2147483648`; wrong code at the ABI/host boundary (High, still-missing piece of §10(1)).

No finding contradicts the 07-09 fixes themselves. All three Highs are *incomplete coverage of paths the fix was supposed to close*, not regressions of correctly-fixed behavior. The new boundary defects (H14 malformed-metadata acceptance, §10 narrow-CAS expected-value) are in code the remediation added.

### Recommended remediation order

1. **Close the M4 `while`-`continue` charging hole** (dedicated charged latch label) and **make charging reach-aware** — these two together make the budget a real bound (closes H-M4-1 and H-M4-2).
2. **Normalize folded integer results to the target type** before the codegen early return — applies to shift *and* add/sub/mul/bitwise (closes H-§10-1).
3. **Centralize canonical-type validation** for v2 `.em` metadata before any allocation (closes M-H14-1) and **mask the atomic-CAS expected operand** to width (closes M-§10-2).
4. **Load `context_t::max_call_depth` through `r14` in B1** depth checks and **trap on null dispatch entries** instead of faulting (closes M-M4-3, M-M4-4).
5. **Restore or document the breaking hot-reload source API** and add an old→new migration section; refresh stale stable docs (closes M-M3-1, M-docs-1).
6. **Reject duplicate current-page publication** across a `HotReloadDomain` (closes M-M3-2).
7. Tighten shift-count signedness/compound rules and bit-preserving conversions (closes the Low + two pre-existing Mediums).

## 2. High findings

### H-M4-1. `while`-`continue` bypasses the loop budget charge

**Evidence:** Reproduced (multiple independent probes, `tmp_edit/c4/`, `tmp_edit/c5/`); 1,000,000-iteration `while (true) { ...; continue; }` completed with `budget=20`.

`WhileStmt` lowers the budget charge *after* the body and makes `continue` target the condition/top label directly, *before* the charge. `for`/`do-while` place their latch labels to include the charge; `while` does not. (`src/codegen.cpp:2377` loop push `{top, end, …}`, `:2381` charge `emit_budget_check(block_cost(ws->body), …)`, `:2382` `jmp top`; `:2490`–`2501` the `ContinueStmt` jump to `loops[i].cont` at `:2497`.)

**Classification:** still-missing piece of fixed M4 (the entry/native accounting shipped; this back-edge hole was not closed). Not a regression — `381ed76`'s loop-only charging had the same hole, but M4's stated objective is a real bound.

**Impact:** an unbounded busy loop with `continue` evades the instruction budget entirely — the safety guarantee M4 was added to provide does not hold on this path.

**Fix (closes the gap):** introduce a dedicated `while` latch label immediately *before* the back-edge charge and make it the `continue` target; the charged latch then jumps to the condition. Add regression tests: finite-vs-infinite `while` with `continue`, nested `switch`-inside-`while` with `continue`, and `continue` after a defer cleanup edge (the M5 transfer interacts with this latch).

### H-M4-2. Coarse per-statement charging cannot bound reachable straight-line work

**Evidence:** Reproduced (`tmp_edit/c4/`, `tmp_edit/c5/`); a single expression of 2,000 terms completed under `budget=5`, 300 emitted native-call sequence under `budget=3`, eight 8 KiB aggregate copies under `budget=30`.

`block_cost`/`stmt_cost` default to a per-AST-statement constant and ignore expression size, native-call-site lowering, argument marshalling, and aggregate byte copies (`src/codegen.cpp:2453-2477`). Entry charging adds a coarse function-entry charge but does not change the per-statement granularity.

**Classification:** still-missing piece of fixed M4.

**Impact:** any reachable straight-line work that is large *within* one statement runs effectively unbilled — the budget does not bound the work it claims to bound.

**Fix (closes the gap):** charge *reached lowered basic blocks* by emitted operation/copy chunks — expressions, argument marshalling, conditions, switch compares, `for` init/step, native-call setup, and aggregate byte copies — not only AST statements. Trusted native body time may remain explicitly outside the unit, but JIT call setup and in-script lowering cannot be free. Add probes: large expression, many native calls, large aggregate copy, all under tight budgets.

### H-§10-1. Narrow typed literal fold bypasses target-width normalization

**Evidence:** Reproduced (`tmp_edit/c5/shift_enum_direct_probe.cpp`, freshly linked against `build_new/libember.a`); a direct host call returned `2147483648` for the folded function while the equivalent runtime function returned `-2147483648` (identical low 32 bits, different required i32 value).

`src/codegen.cpp:1279-1305` returns a folded immediate *before* `normalize_rax(lt)` at `:1420`. Enum lowering produces typed i32 `IntLit` operands (`src/sema.cpp:501-506`), so this path is reachable. `1073741824_i32 << 1` therefore yields zero-extended `0x0000000080000000` instead of sign-normalized `0xffffffff80000000`.

**Classification:** still-missing piece of the fixed §10(1) item. The signed-left-shift *UB* is gone; this is a *wrong-code* remainder at the ABI boundary. Not a regression of a previously correct fold.

**Impact:** wrong i32 results at the host/ABI boundary. The in-language `shift_enum_probe.ember` returns 0 only because i32 comparisons and call-return consumers observe the normalized low width — a circular test that hides the defect. The direct host call exposes it.

**Fix (closes the gap):** before the folded early return, emit the immediate *then* call `normalize_rax(lt)` (or fold/truncate/sign-extend explicitly to `lt`), exactly as the runtime path does. Apply to **every folded integer operation** (Add/Sub/Mul/shift/bitwise share the same early return), not only shift. Add a direct host-call regression using enum i32 sign-bit/overflow values, plus a code-byte or return-value assertion that an i32-only language comparison cannot satisfy.

## 3. Medium findings

### M-H14-1. v2 loader accepts internally inconsistent canonical type metadata

**Evidence:** Reproduced (`tmp_edit/c4/`, `tmp_edit/c5/`); `malformed inconsistent_type accepted=1 pages=1`.

A hand-built v2 type encoded `Prim::Void`, flags=0, but nonempty `struct_name="Ghost"`. The writer derives the struct flag from a nonempty name (`src/em_writer.cpp:89-99`); the loader assigns the name without requiring flag/name equivalence (`src/em_loader.cpp:161-172`) and publishes an executable page.

**Classification:** **new boundary defect introduced by the H14/v2 remediation.** H14's ordinary signature-mismatch rejection itself works; the gap is in canonical-type *shape* validation.

**Fix (closes the gap):** centralize canonical-type validation before any allocation: struct flag iff name nonempty; fn flags iff Fn; recorded signature implies fn; array flag iff nonzero length; slice/array require one valid element; reject impossible primitive/flag combinations recursively. Apply in both writer preflight and loader, with mutation tests for each impossible combination.

### M-§10-2. Narrow atomic CAS does not mask the expected operand to declared width

**Evidence:** Reproduced (`tmp_edit/c4/`); a CAS whose `expected` is out-of-range (or sign-extended beyond width) is compared raw, so a CAS that *should* match a canonical stored value fails (false negative), and an `expected` with stray high bits set could spuriously match.

`extensions/sync/ext_sync.cpp:173-187` masks `desired` to width but compares `expected` exactly. Every other narrow op (new/store/desired/add/sub/swap) canonicalizes its operands to the declared width; CAS alone does not mask the expected value, so a host/script passing an out-of-range expected gets a false success.

**Classification:** **new boundary defect in the pass-2 §10 atomic remediation** (a still-missing consistency piece, not a regression — `381ed76` had the same gap, but the pass-2 rewrite of atomics to `uint64_t` was the moment to close it).

**Fix (closes the gap):** mask `expected` to the declared width before the compare (consistent with how `desired` is masked). Add a probe: 8/16/32-bit CAS with out-of-range expected must fail against a canonical stored value.

### M-M4-3. B1 ignores the per-context `max_call_depth`

**Evidence:** Reproduced (`tmp_edit/c2/m4_probe.cpp`, `tmp_edit/c4/budget_probe.cpp`); compiled with limit 3, a runtime `context_t::max_call_depth` of 100 still trapped, while 1 still allowed one native edge.

`emit_depth_check` compares `call_depth` to `int32_t(ctx.max_call_depth)` (compile-time) in both B1 and baked modes (`src/codegen.cpp:348-363`), never loading `[r14 + context_offsets::max_depth()]`. `context_t::max_call_depth` is public and documented as configured per-context state (`src/context.hpp:60-69`); `context_offsets::max_depth()` exists (`:91-95`) but is unused. The CLI masks this by copying the runtime value into the codegen value before compilation (`examples/ember_cli.cpp:385-396`), so single-context use looks correct.

**Classification:** pre-existing B1 contract mismatch, newly relevant to the M4 combined-depth model.

**Fix (closes the gap):** in B1 mode, compare `call_depth` to `[r14 + context_offsets::max_depth()]` (register-vs-memory, or load max into a scratch); preserve compile-time `CodeGenCtx::max_call_depth` only for legacy baked mode. Add a test: two contexts sharing one compiled body with limits 1 and 100, asserting different behavior. If the intended contract is instead immutable module-wide depth, update `docs/SAFETY_AND_SANDBOX.md` to say so and make the field non-public.

### M-M4-4. Null direct/indirect dispatch entries fault outside Ember traps

**Evidence:** Reproduced in child processes (`tmp_edit/c4/`); a null `dispatch[slot]` produces `0xC0000005` instead of an Ember `TrapReason`.

**Classification:** pre-existing contract/spec gap adjacent to M4.

**Fix (closes the gap):** reject null publication at `DispatchTable::set`/load time, or guard call targets with a null-check trap. Add a test that a null entry produces a recoverable trap rather than a process fault.

### M-M3-1. Old supported single-threaded reload source API removed without migration

**Evidence:** Reproduced compile comparison; `old_api_compile.cpp` exit statuses `381=0, 863=0, HEAD=1`.

`reload_function` at `381ed76`/`863284e` took seven args and returned a caller-owned `old_entry`; at HEAD it takes a `HotReloadDomain&` and the old pointer is gone (`src/hot_reload.hpp:215-239`). Existing hosts using the old signature no longer compile, and the ownership migration is non-mechanical (continuing old cleanup after adding a domain risks double-free).

**Classification:** **regression** relative to both `381ed76` and `863284e` (source API, not stable machine ABI — the helper is inline/header-only).

**Fix (closes the gap):** either ship a deprecated explicitly-single-threaded compatibility abstraction with persistent retirement ownership (a temporary hidden domain is unsafe), or declare a breaking version bump and add a prominent migration recipe: persistent domain beside the table; guard before every slot load; stop freeing/reading `old_entry`; disown the old `CompiledFn`; periodically `reclaim()`/`quiesce()`; drain guards then quiesce before freeing current pages.

### M-M3-2. Duplicate current-page alias can be reclaimed while still published

**Evidence:** Reproduced (`tmp_edit/c2/domain_probe.cpp`, real RX allocation + `VirtualQuery`).

A `HotReloadDomain` is multi-table/multi-slot (`src/hot_reload.hpp:33-35`, `docs/HOT_RELOAD.md:138-139`). `publish` only rejects equality with the *selected* slot (`:101-103`), records that pointer, and updates only that slot (`:105-125`); `reclaim` unconditionally frees eligible records (`:191-203`). Two slots publishing the same allocation, then replacing one, records and reclaims it while another slot still points to it — a guarded call through the second slot then jumps to decommitted memory; replacing both can double-retire/free.

**Classification:** **new M3 boundary surface** (the multi-table domain is new code).

**Fix (closes the gap):** make executable-page ownership move-only and uniquely bound to one `(domain,table,slot)`, rejecting duplicate current publication. If aliases are intentional, maintain domain-wide current-reference counts keyed by allocation and free only at zero current refs *plus* epoch eligibility. Add same-table and two-table alias tests. If uniqueness is contractual, narrow the docs immediately.

### M-docs-1. Stable docs contradict the remediated H12/H14/M4 and name nonexistent lifecycle APIs

**Evidence:** Confirmed by inspection. `docs/GAP_ANALYSIS.md:98-103` says portability/signatures remain redesigns; `docs/MODULES.md:309-322,374-378` describes only v1/no-metadata as the live-link status despite accurate v2 text at `:257-260`; `docs/DESIGN.md:136-144` and `docs/plan_CONTEXT_THREADSAFETY.md:70-83,755-769` still state loop-only budget/script-only depth with stale line citations; `docs/LIFECYCLE.md:24-39,56-68,89-110` presents `ember_get_annotated_functions`, `ember_module_destroy`, generic `ember_call`, and `ember_reload_*` as real APIs (no such facade exists); `docs/BUNDLING_AND_EM_MODULES.md:136` calls single-function reload "unchanged" despite M-M3-1.

**Classification:** still-missing piece of fixed H12/H14/M4 + the M3 migration guidance.

**Fix (closes the gap):** update stable status tables and historical milestone prose to distinguish v1 compatibility from v2 guarantees; refresh line indices; mark lifecycle snippets explicitly as pseudocode or replace with the real `get_annotated_functions`, guarded table load, `ember_call_*`, `reload_function`, and host-owned teardown; add the M-M3-1 migration section and correct "unchanged."

## 4. Low finding

### L-§10-3. Implementation-defined fold/conversion portability

**Evidence:** Confirmed by inspection. The signed-negative right-shift fold (`src/sema.cpp:150`, `src/codegen.cpp:1302`) and out-of-range `uint64_t`→`int64_t` conversions are implementation-defined (not UB). They matched x64 on MinGW but are not portable.

**Classification:** still-missing portability hardening adjacent to §10(1)/(2).

**Fix (closes the gap):** use bit-preserving `memcpy`/C++20 `bit_cast`; implement the arithmetic-right fold as an unsigned shift plus explicit sign fill.

## 5. Verified strengths (re-confirmed independently)

The following were re-checked with fresh probes and should not be reclassified as defects:

1. **07-09 Critical/High fixes hold:** loader bounds/overflow/exception containment (all 5 malformed `.em` fixtures return `ok=0 rc=1`, pages=0, against a freshly-linked probe); cast matrix rejects scalar→slice; nominal handles block `vec3 = 999`; negative array size traps cleanly; `do { return 7 } while(false)` returns 7; div-by-zero and `INT64_MIN/-1` trap (rc=70); `16777217.0 > 16777216.0` is true; `UINT64_MAX > 0` is true; `r13`/`r14` preserved across switch/B1 thunks (fresh `win64_abi` assertions).
2. **H12 portability:** symbolic ordinary-native, operator-native, and function-rodata relocation survives separate producer/consumer processes; missing/incompatible bindings reject before page publication; initialized globals and string rodata round-trip cross-process. The CLI `em_cli_emit` test launches two processes via `system`.
3. **H14 signatures:** ordinary known-signature mismatch (ret/param arity/types) is rejected at link before codegen; v1 remains explicitly ABI-trusted `unknown_sig` (backward compat is real, not a v2-field over-read of v1 bytes).
4. **M3 reclamation core:** the mutex/epoch protocol prevents reclamation of any compliant in-flight guard; `quiesce()` is genuinely blocking and snapshot-scoped; guard moves (construction/assignment/self-move/moved-from destruction) are sound — no double-leave or premature free. 1,000 concurrent guarded publications produced monotonic epochs, correct pinning, and complete retirement.
5. **M4 depth (normal paths):** combined depth is balanced for all direct, indirect, cross-module, helper, overload, hidden-return, and alternate-return paths; longjmp residue is the documented `reset_for_call()` contract, not a sequential leak.
6. **M5 lexical defer:** exact LIFO and exactly-once cleanup across blocks, branches, switches, returns, loops, break/continue; per-iteration reset (no skip/double-fire); i64/narrow/f32/f64/slice/hidden-aggregate returns survive clobbering cleanup calls. Trap/longjmp bypassing later defers is documented and intentional.
7. **§10(2) atomics:** storage and all arithmetic are `uint64_t`/unsigned; every requested operation/width/sign-bit passed under a 30s timeout, including contended narrow add/sub. The original signed-overflow UB is gone.
8. **§10(3) labels:** duplicate `bind()` throws a deterministic `logic_error` ICE at the second bind; unresolved labels still fail at fixup; independent emitters do not false-trigger.
9. **W^X / atomic dispatch / registry-base stability / `PERM_FFI` / provenance guard:** all re-confirmed.

## 6. Trusted-host contracts and documented deferrals (unchanged in substance)

- one `context_t` must not be entered concurrently by multiple threads;
- mutable script globals shared by contexts require host-provided synchronization;
- native bodies are trusted and may violate memory safety if implemented incorrectly;
- natives must obey the Win64 ABI, including `r14` preservation during B1 calls;
- host-driven routine invocation is outside the JIT function-reference guard;
- `PERM_FFI` is a registration policy, not full capability isolation (native slice APIs receive raw addresses by design);
- hot reload is safe with synchronous reclamation only when no concurrent caller can execute the old page (M-M3-2 narrows *when* "no concurrent caller" holds);
- reload/compile mutation of the same `Program` must be serialized by the host;
- trap/`longjmp` recovery bypasses lexical defer cleanup by design (no exception unwinding through JIT frames without Windows unwind metadata);
- `.em` is native x86-64 and is not an authenticated untrusted-code sandbox; v2 adds portability + signature verification but not authentication.

These should be prominent in the embedding API rather than spread across comments.

## 8. Conclusion

The two remediation passes did real work: the 07-09 Critical/High/Medium findings are closed and hold under independent adversarial probing, and the previously-deferred items (H12, H14, M3, M4, M5, §10) are implemented rather than re-documented. Fresh cross-process, concurrency, and direct-JIT probes validated each — the self-tests are not the sole evidence.

The remaining gaps are *incomplete coverage of paths the fixes were supposed to close* (the three Highs: M4 while-continue, M4 coarse charging, §10 narrow fold) plus *new boundary defects in code the remediation added* (H14 malformed-metadata, §10 narrow-CAS, M3 duplicate-alias) plus *integration/documentation regressions* (M3 API removal, stale docs). None is a Critical or a regression of a correctly-fixed 07-09 item. Every finding above is paired with a concrete fix that closes it; the recommended order front-loads the two fixes that together make the M4 budget a real bound and the one fix that closes the wrong-code fold remainder.

After these are addressed, the v1.0 claim is substantially stronger than at `381ed76`: portable v2 artifacts with verified signatures, a reclaimable hot-reload domain, budget/depth accounting that (once the Highs are closed) actually bounds work, lexical-scope defer, and UB-free integer/atomic/emitter internals.

The 07-09 §6.1/§6.3 benchmark-methodology gaps (single mean, no variance/CI, no machine/compiler metadata, `v0.6_BENCHMARK_RESULTS.md` rewritten as a CTest side-effect) are not in this audit's scope because the remediation did not touch `examples/bench_ember_vs_as.cpp` or `v0.6_BENCHMARK_RESULTS.md`; they remain open and should be re-litigated in a future performance-focused audit.
